Meddle

Mobile app redesign

Check out the prototype!

The Challenge


My final project at General Assembly was doing client work for a local company. Meddle specifically requested that we focus on the onboarding and profile creation process to help facilitate thoughtful profiles. Business goals included more time in the app per session and an increase in user accounts.

Team:

John Willis | Jessica Hadley | Elizabeth Wang | Emily Rowley

Duration:

3 Weeks

Roles:

Visual Designer, Interaction Designer, User Research, Animator

Tools:

Adobe Photoshop CC, Sketch, SurveyMonkey, InVision, whiteboarding, paper sketching

Research

Our research started by sending out two screener surveys, which returned over one hundred results. One survey was for online dating, the other about how people set their friends up on dates. Overall, we were able to interview 15 people. I compiled all of the data into one large document and pulled out themes that showed up. As a team, we made an affinity map of the results.

I made two proto personas from the collected data, Michelle and Vanessa. Michelle is our single person, Vanessa is her friend who is looking to help her make a match. Meddle is unique in that as a single person, there's not a lot you can do without someone there to help you pick a match. This leads us to where my main focus was for this project, the tutorial.

Design

The original tutorial that Meddle had was a single picture that tried to convey a really complex process with four different icons all tied together. When researching competitive dating mobile applications, I discovered that most apps used multiple screens or a video to show their process. Meddle's tutorial was buried under two different menus, while other apps had it either when first opening the app or right after creating an account. Initially I did several sketches, of the tutorial and onboarding process. After much discussion and whiteboarding, I took on the task of the tutorial. After much thought and so many iterations, I grabbed my team and proposed the idea of making the tutorial an animation.

My team really liked the idea, so I did some whiteboarding to demonstrate how many keyframes we could deliver in the timeframe.
